A Letter from H.H. Holmes

Dear Alfred,

I hope this letter finds you well. I have been keeping busy with various business ventures and schemes in and around the city. You know me, always finding new ways to make a profit.

However, I must admit that I have taken some risks in my pursuit of wealth. I have acquired a hotel, which I have named the "World's Fair Hotel," and I have been using it for some rather unsavory purposes. The maze of rooms and passages I have created within the hotel has proven to be quite useful in my endeavors.

I must apologize for my recent secrecy, but I have been operating under the radar in order to avoid suspicion. I cannot reveal the full extent of my activities, but I can say that they have been quite lucrative. I trust that you will keep this information to yourself.

As always, I remain your faithful friend and partner.

Sincerely,
H.H. Holmes
